The Golden Shovel of Nature

Write a golden shovel poem — one line at a time

A golden shovel poem takes a line from another poet and uses each word as the last word of each new line of your own poem. Every line you write hides Yeats's words at its end, like seeds buried in soil.

"I have wrapped my dreams in a silken cloth" — W. B. Yeats, He Wishes For The Cloths Of Heaven

9 levels · 9 lines · one buried Yeats line

How to play

Run, jump, and dig your way through nine nature levels. Defeat enemies and break word blocks (jump up into them OR throw your golden shovel) to fill your nature word bank. At each level's end, a boss waits behind a shield. Wait for the shield to drop, dodge its thrown seeds, and land hits — every hit lets you add one word to your line. The line must end with the level's assigned word. Five words minimum. Add more if you like, then choose done to defeat the boss.
